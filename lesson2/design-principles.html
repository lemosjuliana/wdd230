White Space/Clean Design

Stanford d.school

dschool.stanford.edu

Having a white and clean design is one of the most basic design concepts we learn when developing web pages. There are several examples of this principle online, including the most famous: the Google browser. After searching a little more, I came across the Stanford d.school website. I believe this page represents well the concept of Having a white and clean design because it drives appropriate attention to the page elements without filling the page with unnecessary information.

Contrast

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

churchofjesuschrist.org

A well-implemented contrast provides visibility and readability. As a good example, I choose the official website of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Its color pathers are visually appealing and comfortable for reading, and it has no contrast errors, accordingly to the WAVE tool.

Repetition

W3Schools

w3schools.com

Part of creating a website is to predict and study user actions. One of the steps we have to consider is how long it takes for the user to learn how to use the website. Because of that, the repetition principle is fundamental. I particularly like the W3Schools for providing an intuitive environment through repetition and cohesiveness.
